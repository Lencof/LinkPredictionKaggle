Missions

   JPL is the leading U.S. center for robotic exploration of the solar
   system, and has 19 spacecraft and 10 major instruments carrying out
   planetary, Earth science and space-based astronomy missions.

       Past

Asteroid Watch

   Detect, track and characterize potentially hazardous asteroids and
   comets.
   Asteroid Watch
   In 1998, The NASA Near-Earth Object Program Office was established at
   JPL to coordinate NASA-sponsored efforts to detect, track and
   characterize potentially hazardous asteroids and comets that could
   approach the Earth.

Earth-like Planets

What's an Exoplanet?

   A trillion planets in our galaxy alone and many of them in Earth’s size
   range.
   What is an Exoplanet?
   All of the planets in our solar system orbit around the Sun. Planets
   that orbit around other stars are called exoplanets. Click here to see
   how many we have found so far.
   more arrow
   What are CubeSats?

Big ideas, small satellites

What are CubeSats?

   At the forefront of helping redefine the way we do science in space.
   What are CubeSats?
   A CubeSat (U-class spacecraft) is a type of miniaturized satellite for
   space research that is made up of multiples of 10×10×10 cm cubic units.
   Click to learn more.
   more arrow
   What is the Deep Space Network?

Deep Space Network

What is the DSN?

   The largest international array of giant radio antennas supporting
   interplanetary spacecraft missions.
   What is the Deep Space Network?
   The DSN consists of three facilities spaced equidistant from each other
   – approximately 120 degrees apart in longitude – around the world.
   These sites are at Goldstone, near Barstow, California; near Madrid,
   Spain; and near Canberra, Australia. Click to learn more.
