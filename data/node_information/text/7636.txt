Bed Pillows

   Choosing the right bed pillow can help you get a good night's sleep. To
   find the right pillow for you, consider your sleeping habits.

   If you're a belly sleeper, look for an extra-soft pillow so that your
   neck and head aren't higher than your back. A soft pillow will allow
   your head and neck to sink into the pillow, aligning your neck with
   your spine.

   If you're a back sleeper, you'll want a pillow that's soft, but not as
   soft as one for a belly sleeper. This will support your head enough to
   keep it level with your neck and spine.

   If you're a side sleeper, your head and neck will typically be higher
   than your body. To align your neck, head and spine, you'll need a firm
   pillow.

   Cotton and polyester-fill pillows are popular options that can range
   from soft to firm. Down pillows are very soft and often cool to the
   touch, while memory foam pillows are designed to conform to the shape
   of your head and neck for better support.

   At Walmart, you'll find a wide variety of bed pillows, all at Every Day

Sheds

   When it comes to making the most of your outdoor space, outdoor
   furniture is a popular choice for many, whether it's a sofa so you can
   relax and enjoy a good book, or a dining set to entertain guests and
   family. Storage, however, is another important option to consider, as
   it helps to keep your outdoor space organized. At Walmart, you'll find
   a great selection of sheds to choose from, all at Every Day Low Prices.
   Here is a quick, easy guideline so you can find the right one for your
   outdoor space.

Advantages of owning a shed

   Sheds protect valuable gardening equipment like shovels, rakes and
   hedge trimmers. Garden tools that are kept out of the weather will last
   longer and perform better when you need them. A large enough shed can
   also store lawn mowers, string trimmers, chainsaws and other power
   equipment. In addition to protecting your gardening gear, a shed can
   also make your property look tidier because everything is enclosed in
   its place, rather than leaning in a corner or cluttering your garage.

Choosing the right size

   Measure your garden or deck space, your tools and then consider the
   measurements of the sheds or storage boxes in your range. Choose a shed
   that will accommodate your largest tools or equipment and still fit in
   the area you plan to locate it. A bit of extra space will almost always
   be used when it comes to storing your garden tools.

Carports

   Provide your vehicle with some protection from the elements with a
   carport. Think about the size you need for the type of vehicle you have
   and your preferred style.

   Size: Choose the size to suit your needs as well as the space you have
   available. Consider the size of your vehicle, whether you want
   additional covered space to work on it, whether you want to provide
   cover for more than one vehicle and if you need room to store other
   items.

   Style: An open design gives you more room to move around, but a closed
   design provides better protection against the elements. Compromise with
   removable walls. In extreme weather conditions, opt for a snow shed
   design. Windows in the canvas walls provide natural lighting whereas
   sidewalls without windows provide better privacy.
